## D) Strategic Implications (Synthesis)

- **Agent competition is shifting from model quality alone to full workflow ownership.** Products that combine coding, deployment, collaboration, and distribution loops are gaining narrative advantage.
- **Licensing and provenance are becoming core product risks, not legal footnotes.** Any perceived mismatch between marketing claims and model lineage can quickly become a high-visibility trust event.
- **Developer adoption is increasingly subsidy-driven at the top of funnel.** Student credits, referral campaigns, and buildathons indicate an aggressive race for default user behavior before workflows harden.
- **MCP/tool-augmentation is emerging as a force multiplier for experts.** The highest-signal demonstrations now involve model + toolchain integration rather than standalone prompting.
- **Open-source agent momentum is materially rising.** Strong HN response to OpenCode and related tooling suggests buyers want optionality versus closed, expensive coding stacks.
- **Policy and platform control points are tightening simultaneously.** Wikipedia governance debates and App Store distribution frictions suggest AI builders face a more constrained operating environment in public channels.
