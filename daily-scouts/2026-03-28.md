B) What happened:

Net effect: more dynamic control over how evaluation runs and how failures/retries are handled.
C) Why this matters:
First-order: teams can encode reliability policies directly into eval loops (retry semantics, fallback behavior, model-routing decisions), which shortens the gap between offline benchmark logic and production orchestration.

D) My take:
This is a quiet but important shift from “typed outputs” tooling toward “typed operations” tooling. The tradeoff is complexity: once eval hooks can mutate model choice, reproducibility can suffer unless run metadata and deterministic seeds are rigorously captured.
E) What to watch next:
Watch for ecosystem patterns around standardized hook libraries (retry taxonomies, budget-aware routing, policy packs). Also watch for built-in provenance tooling so eval runs remain auditable when dynamic routing is enabled.
